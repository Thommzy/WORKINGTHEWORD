CHRIST OUR WISDOM

Posted by DAVID O OLATUNJI on May 30, 2018
“But of him are ye in Christ Jesus, who of God is made unto us wisdom, and righteousness, and sanctification and redemption”- 1 Corinthians 1:30 (KJV)

The Greek word for Wisdom is Sophia which emphasizes on understanding of ultimate things such as life and death, God and man, righteousness and sin, heaven and hell, eternity and time. Sophia is mental excellence in its highest and fullest sense.




Spiritual wisdom is given only by the Holy Spirit. In the Old Testament, Solomon exemplified this wisdom When Jesus also came on the face of the earth, His wisdom also surpassed the wisdom of the wisest among men. This wisdom was seen in the Lord Jesus even when He was a small boy. He displayed exemplary wisdom to his elders and teachers. When leaders were to be appointed in the church of Jerusalem, the apostles selected men who possessed this spiritual wisdom.



The scripture above says that Christ is made our wisdom. A child of God does not have his or her on wisdom but Christ’s wisdom. The exact wisdom that is at work in Christ is what is at work in a believer. But do we know this and are we living our lives by this truth? Because we still find ourselves occasionally saying “I don’t know what to do or I’m confused”. This is not true; for it to be we need to imagine Jesus Christ not knowing what to do or being confused.


The Gospels- Matthew, Mark, Luke and John are books that fully display the wisdom of Christ. He always knew what to do; He always knew what to say. They brought all kinds of sick people to Jesus but He wasn’t healing them the same way. To the blind, he used mud; to the leprous he says go, wash yourself in a river.


We are not to live our lives independent of the wisdom of Christ in us. Allow Him to guide you today in every decision you are making and every project you are handling. Say it to yourself as many times in a day until it sinks in that you have no wisdom of your own so everything that you do or say is right. The next time you find yourself in a challenging situation, remember, Christ is your wisdom.


Confession:

I acknowledge that the wisdom of Christ is at work in me. I have the mind of Christ. I always know what to say, I always know what to do. The Wisdom of Christ directs me in everything I do every day.
