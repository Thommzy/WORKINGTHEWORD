THAT I MAY KNOW HIM

Posted by DAVID O OLATUNJI on May 22, 2018
“(For my determined purpose is) that I may know Him (that I may progressively become more deeply and intimately acquainted with Him, perceiving and recognizing and understanding the wonders of His person more strongly and more clearly), and that I may in that same way come to know the power overflowing from his resurrection (which it exerts over believers), and that I may so share His sufferings as to be continually transformed (in spirit into His likeness even) to His death (in the hope)-Philippians 3:10 (AMP)

An effective walk with God is born out of a continuous, daily desire to know Him more and more and go deeper in fellowship with Him. Christianity is definitely not a religion; it is not an embodiment of rules that we must work at keeping in order to climb the ladder to heaven. Rather, it is a personal, growing relationship with the risen, living Lord Jesus Christ that results in our being more and more like Him. Our goal is to know Him and to become like Him. Paul wrote all of the epistles, had great revelations about Jesus and His will but He still desired to know more.




Prayer is beyond communication to God; it is our weapon of change on the face of the earth. However, the change that you seek is tied to your belief that you receive it when you ask for it. Jesus Christ made it a principle that when you pray, believe that you have received and you will see the results. Our confidence is in the name of Jesus. The name of Jesus is our power of attorney to do anything and have anything. The Bible says “Therefore God has highly exalted Him and given Him a name that is above every name, that at the mention of the name of Jesus, every knee should bow of things in heaven and things on earth, and of things under the earth”- Philippians 2:9-10.



Jesus said the same thing when He prayed, “And this is eternal life, that they may know you, the only true God, and Jesus Christ whom you have sent” (John 17:3). Christianity is primarily a growing relationship with the infinite God who has revealed Himself through the Lord Jesus Christ.


You need to know Christ personally. You can know a lot about someone without knowing the person himself. Like any relationship, once you’ve met, you must cultivate that relationship by spending time getting to know them, what they like and do not like, what they can do and cannot do. It’s the same in a personal relationship with Christ. It requires cultivation and that requires time. Do you often make time to spend with the Lord? We should spend our lives focused on that great goal, “to know Him.” But it won’t happen if you aren’t committed to becoming a man or woman of the Word.


Confession

I am committed to spending my time and life in the Word of God. It is my desire and purpose to know Him and be more like Him. Amen.
